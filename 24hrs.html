24 hours without Technology



My attempt at not using a screen had been an unfortunate failure. I had planned to not use my phone for the entirety of Sunday October 27th. My day started with me attempting to head to work. I had left for work feeling confident I would be able to make it through the day.

I found myself stuck in heavy traffic on my way to work, I needed to notify my workplace I would be late so I HAD to use my phone to notify them. My manager had alerted me that there had been a mudslide on I5 north that had accumulated over the previous night causing the highway to be closed down for 4 exits and apparently it was explicitly announced online about the highway closure causing me to not be informed of the issue. I also needed my phone to reroute my drive to my workplace.

Once I got to work I effectively had not been using my phone. I had felt a strong desire to use my phone on my lunch due to my normal routine to pass time using my phone. To fight this urge I decided to draw in the breakroom. Although this was helpful, I have a small attention span (possibly due to my attachment to technology) making my investment in creating creative work to be lacking. I also draw very fast, consisting of a lot of sketches so I found myself getting bored of a centralized activity.

By the time I had left work the road closure was resolved.
